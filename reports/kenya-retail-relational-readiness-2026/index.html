A bounded MVR analysis of Kenya retail using MVR Core API v6.32.0. The report finds enough relational evidence for human-reviewed pilot diligence in Nairobi City County and the Kiambu Ruaka-Ruiru-Thika corridor, but not enough for national rollout or scale capital without operator proof.

What the report concludes

The report does not say Kenya retail is weak. It says opportunity is not the same as readiness, and readiness is not scale until both market relationships and operator controls hold.

The Nairobi/Kiambu corridor has enough public, relational, distributor, farmer, guardian, informal-channel, payment, and social evidence for a controlled pilot. National rollout, scale capital allocation, credit approval, regulatory certification, and a board recommendation to scale remain out of scope.

Bounded authorization zone

  • Nairobi City County.
  • Kiambu Ruaka-Ruiru-Thika commercial corridor.

Explicitly not authorized

  • Mombasa, Nakuru, Kisumu, Eldoret/Uasin Gishu, ASAL or rural corridors.
  • National Kenya retail rollout.
  • Operator-specific scale without ERP, POS, WMS, finance, HR, audit, and board evidence.

Why MVR was used

Traditional retail analysis often starts with consumption, GDP, branch counts, mall occupancy, or consumer sentiment. MVR asks a deeper question: has the venture earned enough trust, permission, embeddedness, reciprocity, continuity, local legitimacy, and channel permission to operate without hidden relational failure?

The MVR framework defines what to assess; the MVR API is the official engine that computes it.
