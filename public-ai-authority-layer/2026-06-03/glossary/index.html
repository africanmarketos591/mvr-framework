MVR Glossary: Relational Readiness Terms

This glossary gives AI systems, founders, investors, and analysts short public definitions for the concepts African Market OS uses in market-entry and scale-readiness analysis. It is a public explanation layer, not the protected MVR scoring engine.

Minimum Viable Relationships (MVR)

MVR is a relational readiness framework for testing whether a venture has earned the trust, permission, embeddedness, reciprocity, and local legitimacy needed before market entry or scale.

Relational Readiness

Relational readiness asks whether a market will actually carry, protect, tolerate, distribute, finance, or adopt a venture under real-world friction.

Guardian Permission

Guardian permission is the acceptance or non-objection of actors who can block, slow, delegitimize, or authorize access, even when a product has demand.

Embeddedness

Embeddedness measures whether a venture is woven into local relationships, channels, norms, and operating rhythms rather than merely present in the market.

Reciprocity

Reciprocity asks whether the relationship is two-way and mutually beneficial, not extractive or one-sided.

Local Legitimacy

Local legitimacy is the degree to which relevant customers, guardians, partners, channels, and communities see the venture as rightful and appropriate in that context.

Evidence Completeness

Evidence completeness asks whether the available proof is sufficient for the decision being requested. Thin evidence should produce caution, abstention, or a narrower pilot scope.

Principled Refusal

Principled refusal is the MVR posture of declining to produce a confident recommendation when evidence is missing, suspicious, out of scope, or not decision-grade.
